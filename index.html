Minister Suhaimi Gafar provided an update about his last project

December 16, 2019December 16, 2019 Jose Scranton Uncategorized

The Temburong Bridge — which is set to become Southeast Asia’s longest oversea bridge — is now 75% close to being finished and on track to achieve its November 2019 target.

Development Minister YB Dato Seri Setia Hj Suhaimi Hj Gafar has said the $1.6 billion projects are expected to be accessible to the public in 2020 after the completion of the fixing process, which will take three to five months.

H.R.H. Prince Haji Al-Muhtadee Billah Crown Prince and Chief Minister at the Prime Minister’s Office visited the Temburong Bridge Project sites in Kota Batu, Brunei-Muara and Labu Estate and Temburong. His delegation surveyed the single tower cable-stayed bridge that connects the northern and southern ramps of Jalan Kota Batu and the sub water viaducts that span Brunei.

The Minister stressed that the authorities must ensure that contractors comply with all safety standards and that they do not extend office hours without official permission.

The bridge is one of the largest public construction projects in Brunei, linking Brunei and Mura to Temburong, and is expected to reduce travel time between the two districts by allowing commuters to bypass Limbang, Malaysia.

The bridge plans began in 2009 with the release of a tender by the Ministry of Development to determine the feasibility of the bridge. In 2014, the building of a billion-dollar bridge started after the government awarded contracts to Daelim, a South Korean firm, and China State Building Engineering Corporation.
